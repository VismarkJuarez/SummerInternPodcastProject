The Codigo Initiative was formed in 2020 with the aim of closing the socio-economic opportunity divide in STEM. To this end, the Codigo Initiative Scholarship Fund was established to support current undergraduate university/college students from underrepresented groups pursuing majors in STEM-related fields. The scholarships (worth $500 USD each) will be awarded to students who possess a history of overcoming adversity, and a commitment to helping improve the lives of others through self-less service to his/her community.

To be considered for the Codigo Initiative Scholarship, you must be currently enrolled in an accredited college or university within the United States--including Alaska or U.S Territory--and must submit a copy of your unofficial college/university transcripts, along with a brief 500 word essay. The essay prompt and application are located in the section below:

Please write a 500 word essay response to the prompt below. You may write the essay directly in the text area below, or copy and paste it in from your favorite text editor.

The National Association of Manufacturing reports that, by the year 2025, more than 2 million American STEM jobs are likely to go unfilled due to a lack of skilled STEM workers. This issue is further compounded by the low participation of women, people of color, and economically disadvantaged individuals in STEM-related fields. Write about what you have done in service to your community, and what you hope to do in the future to help close the opportunity gap in STEM fields for underrepresented groups.
