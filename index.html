The Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago is one of twelve regional Reserve Banks that, along with the Board of Governors in Washington, D.C., make up the United States's central bank. The Chicago Reserve Bank serves the Seventh Federal Reserve District, which encompasses the northern portions of Illinois and Indiana, southern Wisconsin, the Lower Peninsula of Michigan, and the state of Iowa.

The following podcast series aims to inform the general public, as well as Federal Reserve Bank employees, on the narratives, goals and state of economic and monetary policy from within the bank itself.

Pilot Episode

Community & Economic Development During Pandemic

On this episode, a member of the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago's Community & Economic Development team discusses how businesses have coped with the economic turmoil brought upon by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Episode 1

Interview with FRB's Chief Economist on the Economic Effects of COVID19 for Chicago and the U.S at-large

On this episode, we interview a chief economic strategist in the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago to learn more about the economic toll the city--and the country--can expect to see throughout the span of the ongoing COVID-19 recession.

Episode 2

Interview with FRB's President

On this episode, we sit down with the president of the Chicago Federal Reserve Bank to discuss the bank's current state of affairs, short term objectives, and long-term goals. The president also addresses pre-collected questions posed by Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago employees.

Episode 3

Interview with FRB's Chief Network Security Officer

With the Federal Reserve Bank's FedNow service expected to be complete by 2024, computer networking concerns have become a top priority for FedNow developers and project managers. On this episode, we sit down with a chief network security officer at the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago to discuss FedNow, and the security measures being taken in order to deliver fast, secure and reliable end-to-end money transfering capabilities.
